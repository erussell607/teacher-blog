Goal/s of the lesson

By the end of the lesson, students will be able to tell time to the hour and half hour on a digital and analog clock.

PART II: WRITE THE OBJECTIVE/S FOR THE LESSON

Specific learning target(s) / objectives:

*** Objectives have a formal structure and differ in that way from the Goals you stated above.

 

Students will have the ability to correctly identify what the hour and a half hour are on the clock when pointing “exactly” to the 12 and 6. Students will be able to decipher the hour from the minutes on a digital clock, including the number to the left of the colon, the number right of the colon, and the purpose of the colon. Students will state time in words using “o’clock” as well as reading and writing time numerically as “:00” or “:30.”

PART III TEACHING THE LESSON


A. Opening/Introduction

 (also referred to as a “motivator” or “hook”)

 
  •  ENGAGE- Teacher will read IQ It’s Time to the class at the carpet


B. Procedures 

** Include enough detail so that someone else could replicate your lesson. Unless your instructor directs you otherwise, this section should read almost like a script.

 

 
  • EXPLORE-  (with engage-) While reading IQ It’s Time, students will follow along and show the times mentioned throughout the story on their clocks.

  • EXPLAIN- Teacher will have a large analog and digital clock on the interactive smart board. She will remind the students that clocks tell the hours and minutes, and some even show seconds. The teacher will ask the students to tell her which hand is the hour hand, and which one tracks minutes. Next, they will think back to the story IQ It’s Time and discuss a few events that happen throughout the story, and we will display some of those times on the board.

  • ELABORATE- Musical Clocks- Give each student a blank clock paper and a recording sheet in which they will record the time they put on their clock at the top, and have blank spaces to record each additional clock. Each student will draw hands on their clock (hour and minute) and leave it on their desk. The teacher will play music, and the students dance around, moving from desk to desk with their recording sheet. When the music stops, students record whose desk they stopped at and the time on their clock. Repeat until they have recorded every student’s time. At the end of the activity they turn in their clock and recording sheet so the teacher can assess their time-telling skills.
